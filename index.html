All About Mangoes

Overview

Mangoes are a tropical fruit that belong to the Anacardiaceae family, which also includes cashews and pistachios. They are native to South Asia but are now grown in many parts of the world, including Central and South America, Africa, and the Caribbean. The scientific name of the mango is Mangifera indica.

Health Benefits

Mangoes are not only delicious but also nutritious. They are:

  • Rich in vitamin C, which is important for immune function, wound healing, and iron absorption
  • A good source of dietary fiber, which can help regulate digestion and lower the risk of heart disease
  • High in antioxidants, which can protect against cellular damage and inflammation
  • May have anti-inflammatory and anticancer properties due to the presence of phytochemicals like mangiferin and quercetin

How to Enjoy Mangoes

Mangoes can be enjoyed in a variety of ways, such as:

  1. Eating them fresh, sliced or cubed
  2. Blending them into smoothies or juices
  3. Adding them to fruit salads
  4. Incorporating them into savory dishes like salsa, curry, or stir-fry
  5. Baking them into pies, cakes, and tarts
  6. Drying them into chewy mango slices as a snack
  7. Making them into mango chutney or jam
